John Lennon

Vocals

Known for his distinct nasal voice and indelible impact on popular music, there are no Beatles if not for John Lennon. Sharing songwriting and lead vocal duties with Paul McCartney, the Quarrymen, a band which a teenage Lennon started in 1956, was the original Beatles group. Lennon took on the face of the Beatles in the early years, but gradually sunk into the background despite his contributions.

As the Beatles began to drift apart in the late sixties, Lennon met Japanese artist Yoko Ono. The two married in 1969 and enjoyed a fruitful partnership that included musical collaborations, the most popular of which being the Plastic Ono Band.

An outspoken activist, largely against the Vietnam War, Lennon was a figure of controversy in some respects, with reports of his demeaning attitude towards women. He and Ono split in 1973 and Lennon took a hiatus from music lasting from 1975 to 1980. In 1980, he and Ono reconnected.

In a tragic turn of events, on December 8th, 1980, Lennon was murdered by fan Mark David Chapman while returning to his Manhattan apartment, shot from behind at close range. His legacy lives on, though, as an international symbol of peace and artistic innovation.

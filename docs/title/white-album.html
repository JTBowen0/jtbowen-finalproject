The Beatles ("The White Album")

Easily the Beatles' lengthiest and most eclectic album, the self-titled album, widely known as the White Album, stands as a divisive landmark in the Beatles history. Facing pressure to release a project despite internal strife and a mutual feeling of disbandment, the White Album shows the band pushing the envelope of the genre. With experimental tracks like Wild Honey Pie and Revolution 9 mixed with soulful ballads like Blackbird, the White Album offers a range of sounds, some beloved and some dismissed.

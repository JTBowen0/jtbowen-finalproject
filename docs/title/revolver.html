Revolver

Reflecting the band's continued dive into narcotics, this time around LSD, Revolver stands as an innovative album and maybe the Beatles' first true classic. Revolver has it all, with Love You To's use of the sitar displaying George Harrison's infatuation with Indian culture, Paul's knack for beautiful and dreamy love songs in Here, There And Everywhere, John's slithering I'm Only Sleeping and And Your Bird Can Sing, a perfect pop song that clocks in at a neat two minutes, the project is stylistically-different but flows perfectly. Eleanor Rigby coming from a group of guys in their early twenties in the year 1966 is just unfathomable, and Tomorrow Never Knows is an excellent foray into experimental music from the boys. Terrific and worthy of all the praise.

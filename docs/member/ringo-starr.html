Ringo Starr

Drums

Richard Starkey, better known as Ringo Starr, was the last of the core four to join the Beatles and served as the band’s drummer. While oft-overlooked as the least talented or important of the group, Starr remains a talented drummer whose style set the table for the band’s success.

Starr joined the Beatles officially in August 1962 after filling in occasionally in 1960. He replaced drummer Pete Best, who was dismissed by manager Brian Epstein. He quickly became a fan favorite, and was regarded for his charm and charisma in the public eye. Starr sang typically on one song per record, with Octopus’s Garden on Abbey Road and With A Little Help From My Friends on Sgt. Pepper’s known to be some of his better performances.

Starr has gone on to have a prolific career since leaving the Beatles, largely with Ringo Starr and His All-Starr Band. He was knighted in 2018, and joins McCartney as the only living Beatles.

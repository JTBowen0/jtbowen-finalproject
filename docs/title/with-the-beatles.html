With the Beatles (1963)

The Beatles' second album features much of the same from their first, with little growth. All My Loving is a fun and bouncy track, and George Harrison's Don't Bother Me is cool as an early indicator towards the path he'll eventually split off on, but largely songs that follow the same doo-wop formula. Nothing bad, but not much to write home about, either. The world in 1963 would vehemently disagree.
